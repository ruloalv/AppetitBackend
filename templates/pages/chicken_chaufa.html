Chicken Chaufa

  • Yield: Serves 4
  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes

Chicken Chaufa is a popular Peruvian-Chinese fried rice dish that's quick and easy to make. It's packed with flavor and can be customized with your favorite ingredients. This recipe serves 4 people and is perfect for a delicious weeknight dinner.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups cooked white rice, cold
  • 2 chicken breasts, diced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup green bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots mix
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

DIRECTIONS

  1. In a large wok or skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced chicken and cook until it's browned and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the wok and set it aside.
  2. Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in the wok. Add the minced garlic, red bell pepper, green bell pepper, and frozen peas and carrots mix. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the wok and pour the beaten eggs into the other side. Scramble the eggs until they are cooked through.
  4. Add the cold, cooked rice to the wok and stir-fry for about 5 minutes, breaking up any clumps.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the wok and stir to combine.
  6. Drizzle the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil over the rice mixture. Stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes or until everything is well combined and heated through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Chicken Chaufa!

TIP:

Feel free to add your favorite vegetables or protein to customize this dish to your liking.
